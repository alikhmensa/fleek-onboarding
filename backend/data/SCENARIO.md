# Demo scenario

## The demo user: "StreetWear Vault" (Shiv's Shopify dev store)

Seeded by `seller-integration/seed_shopify.py` on the Shiv branch: branded streetwear
— Nike, Adidas, Carhartt WIP, Stussy, Palace, Levi's, The North Face, New Balance —
listed at £45–195. Current stock is heavy on **jackets, hoodies and sneakers**.

What the pipeline should infer for this seller:
- aesthetic: streetwear / 90s sportswear / branded vintage
- price band: roughly £45–120
- oversupplied: branded jackets & hoodies (their whole current wall)
- plausible gaps: knitwear, denim, accessories, tees — categories their buyers
  wear but they barely stock

**⚠ Shiv's seed script creates PRODUCTS ONLY — no orders.** Price band and budget
come from order history, so the store must also get orders:
`SHOP_DOMAIN=... SHOPIFY_ADMIN_TOKEN=... python -m scripts.populate_shop` (our
script backdates paid orders over 10 weeks), or the demo user uploads
`data/streetwear_vault_orders.xlsx` during onboarding — same persona, generated
by `scripts/generate_demo_xlsx.py` (re-run before the demo to freshen dates).

## The second persona: demo_orders.csv (budget Y2K seller)

`data/demo_orders.csv` — Y2K / 90s sportswear / Carhartt at £22–62. Gaps: knitwear,
footwear. This gives judges an out-of-the-box contrast: same flow, cheaper shop,
different bundles.

## The inventory (data/inventory.json, 200 items)

Generated by `scripts/generate_inventory.py`. Design goals:

1. **Winnable for both personas**: every likely gap category (knitwear, footwear,
   denim, accessories, tees) has items ≥3× margin in BOTH price tiers — budget
   (resale £25–60) and premium (resale £60–150).
2. **Filter has real work to do**: ~1/3 of items sit below 3× margin, some resale
   prices sit outside any given band — economics filtering visibly removes items.
3. **Different inputs → different bundles**: 10 categories × 2 price tiers ×
   8 suppliers. A £30-median seller and a £80-median seller retrieve different
   candidates, pass different band filters, and bundle from different suppliers.
4. A handful of "grail" outliers (Moncler, Evisu, Margiela...) that should almost
   never be recommended — proof the filter works when judges ask.

Suppliers: s_01 Northern Knits (knitwear, MOQ 4) · s_02 Sole Archive (footwear, 3)
· s_03 Duck & Dungaree (workwear, 5) · s_04 Terrace Classics (sportswear, 4)
· s_05 Indigo House (denim, 3) · s_06 Peak Outerwear (outerwear, 3)
· s_07 Rosewood Vintage (dresses/accessories, 4) · s_08 Archive Street Supply
(hoodies/tees/grails, 4).

After regenerating: `python -m scripts.seed_inventory` re-embeds and re-upserts
Pinecone (+ local fallback).
